MUʻO

The young leaf bud beginning to open.

At Purple Maiʻa, they are the younger members of our ʻohana, the next generation of builders, designers, and overall talent. Like all muʻo, they need the right conditions, people, spaces, and moments to really blossom. By welcoming them into your spaces, you showed our muʻo that people who look like them, and come from where they come from, hold space in these incredible companies, ultimately showing them that there is a place for them in this industry too.

What follows is a day-by-day account of the week.

Day 1 apple
Eddie brings such a fun energy. It was also cool because he gave us his undivided attention the whole time.

Day 1 — Apple

Eddie welcomed us to Apple's Cupertino campus, and the intentionality was immediate in the architecture and design of the company that pioneered UX/UI. Our muʻo reflected on Eddie's "becoming journey" and appreciated his practical breakdown of the skills and demands tied to each tech role. The candid conversation about AI shaping the workforce — the disruption, the opportunity, and what actually matters for the next generation — was exactly what they needed to hear. Learning about Eddie's proximity to features used daily on the iPhone, and his recollection of meetings with Tim Cook, was pretty epic as well.

To close, Eddie sent us off with turmeric energy shots, a welcomed boost heading into the rest of the week.

Later that evening, we celebrated Mokihana's 21st birthday at the iconic Original Joe's, in the birthday section that went off every five minutes. The butter cake, if you haven't had it, was something else. We got it twice over the span of the trip, which is saying something.

Day 2 salesforce
+ elemental

Day 2 — Salesforce + Elemental Interactive

On day 2 we got to check out the famous goliath occupying San Francisco's downtown. Upon entry we unknowingly stepped into an amusement park ride, shooting 1,070 feet above the city into the ʻOhana Floor, where 360-degree views of the Bay Area stunned our virgin eyes.

The energy up there was unlike anything we experienced at any other company — busy, young, and alive. And then there was the rooftop garden! ʻĀina mid-city, yes please.

Mahalo Arlene for a visit that had a little bit of everything — it was a perfect precursor to our time at Elemental Interactive, where we got to watch pitches and learn more about startup culture.

We ended the night at Pacific Cocktail Haven (trust me, get the Thrilla in Manila) — a decided must — and walked a few blocks uphill to The Public Izakaya for the best karaage chicken and worst ramen of our lives, mainly because Victor's ramen had potato chips on top…

Day 3 lucasfilm
The experiences of the Lucasfilm team were, like, crazy.

Day 3 — Lucasfilm

Third year visiting the Lucasfilm campus - hosted again by Jay - and it does not get old. The wall art and foreign film posters alone deserve their own walking tour, never mind the memorabilia lining every hallway. Paradise.

The campus tour, the film screening, and the conversation that followed all pointed in the same direction: perseverance — and apparently some light stalker tendencies. One muʻo said, "I think I laughed more during this visit than in my entire three years living up here." Another noted how refreshing it was to find that the people working at such a high level are genuinely down to earth and invested in offering really practical advice.

After the visit we made our way to Chinatown to soak up a bit of culture, but most things were closing — so we tracked down egg tarts and ate them on the sidewalk, surrounded by street art and trash cans piled high. It felt authentic, a little like home, but tasted a bit better.

Post-Chinatown we missioned to Sunset for French cuisine at Galette — noted as the favorite meal of the trip. I highly recommend their roasted chicken with mushroom sauce, the brie burger, and the beef tartare. Take me back. Also worth mentioning: we started our day at Cinderella's — another culinary win.

Day 4 google
Google was my highlight company because we were able to talk with them one-on-one.

Day 4 — Google

Lyn opened with news that a muʻo from last year's trip was starting at Google, and just like that, eyes were wide and possibilities palpable. The group's unofficial slogan for the rest of the day was pretty much "open to work… only for Google." Which feels on trend considering the Kamehameha Schools alumni already there, thriving and holding it down at one of the most recognized companies in the world. Their input on adaptability was top-notch, and so were their stories of hustle in young startups. One muʻo mentioned Google was their highlight because of the one-on-one conversations and how easy it was to relate to the people in the room.

But let's not forget the most important moment of the day: Jaden ordering the "hah-leh kah-leh" salad.

Mahalo everyone at Google for the warmth, the aloha, and for showing our muʻo what the path can look like when someone like them walks it well.

Day 5 meta
Honestly, I loved all of these companies.

Day 5 — Meta

Welcome to Disneyland. Jk — but the Meta campus was designed by Frank Gehry, the same architect, and the vibes were committed.

This was our first time meeting Jeremiah and Jon but they welcomed us warmly and had the utmost patience walking us through a massive campus in our very special "business-colored" badges. We loved the retro Japanese arcade machines, the music room, and the free froyo. But beyond the perks, what stuck was the intention behind it all — along with the iterations baked into each improvement. It sort of felt like a physical representation of the path of so many people we spoke to over the course of the trip. Minus Jeremiah, who is ride or die Meta.

Walking through V1 of the new buildings we stopped at a poster that read "building the future of connection." Curiosity piqued — and expecting an answer that involved a virtual metaverse — I asked Jeremiah what he thought the future of connection looked like. He said something along the lines of exactly what we were doing right then: being in person, together, building community. It landed — especially in Meta.

I hope you catch a glimpse of those foxes, guys.
